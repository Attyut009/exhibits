Geography: Aryabhatta Satellite
1. Aryabhatta satellite was India's first satellite.
2. It was launched on April 19, 1975.
3. It was named after the ancient mathematician Aryabhatta.
4. The satellite focused on X-ray astronomy and solar physics.
5. It marked the beginning of India's space program.
English: Direct and Indirect Speech
1. Direct speech quotes the exact words of the speaker.
2. Indirect speech reports what someone said without quoting directly.
3. Tense changes often occur when converting from direct to indirect speech.
4. Pronouns and time expressions may also need adjustment.
5. Learning both forms improves writing and communication skills.
Bengali: Somas
1. Somas are an important literary topic in Bengali studies.
2. They involve understanding classical poetry and prose forms.
3. Students analyze the structure and meaning of Somas.
4. It helps improve cultural and language knowledge.
5. Projects include presentation and textual interpretation.
Physics: Automatic Street Light
1. Automatic street lights turn on/off based on light levels.
2. A sensor detects sunlight and triggers the circuit.
3. LEDs are commonly used for energy efficiency.
4. The system saves electricity by only lighting at night.
5. Students design and demonstrate the working prototype.
Biology: Respiratory System
1. The respiratory system allows gas exchange in humans.
2. Lungs, trachea, bronchi, and alveoli are key organs.
3. Oxygen is taken in, carbon dioxide expelled.
4. Diagrams and models help in understanding the system.
5. The project demonstrates breathing mechanism and health tips.
Computer: Star Topology and Website
1. Star topology connects all computers to a central hub.
2. It is easy to manage and troubleshoot.
3. The project demonstrates data flow in the network.
4. Students also create a website for project presentation.
5. Combines theory and practical implementation skills.
Chemistry: Sedimentation
1. Sedimentation is the process of settling of particles in a liquid.
2. It separates solid and liquid components.
3. Used in water purification and laboratory experiments.
4. Students observe and record the rate of sedimentation.
5. The project explains scientific principles behind the process.
